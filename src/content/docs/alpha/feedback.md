Writing apps live or die on whether they fit real writers' real workflows — something only
real writers can tell us. Every piece of feedback during this alpha shapes the version the
rest of the world will see at 1.0.

The best place to report issues is the project's **GitHub Issues** page. If you were given a
private email or chat channel with your build, you can use that too.

## What to include in a bug report

When filing a bug, please include:

- Your operating system and version
- The MUSE version (visible in the About dialog or the window title)
- What you were doing when the problem happened
- What you expected to happen
- What actually happened
- Any error messages, if they appeared
- Whether you can reproduce it reliably

Screenshots and short screen recordings are enormously helpful.

:::danger[Data-loss bugs come first]
If you lose work or suspect you might have, report it immediately — and **do not overwrite
the backup folders next to your `.muse` project.** Data-loss issues are our highest priority.
:::

## General feedback is welcome too

Bugs aren't the only useful feedback. If something is confusing, slow, awkward, or just
doesn't fit how you work, we want to hear it. First impressions on first launch are
especially valuable and easy to forget later — jot them down as they happen.
