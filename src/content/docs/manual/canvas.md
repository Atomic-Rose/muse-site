---
title: The Canvas
description: A free-form spatial board for arranging scene cards, grouping them, and drawing connections.
---

The **Canvas** is the Scene Board's spatial mode — an open, pannable space where you can
place scene cards anywhere, cluster them into groups, add notes, and draw connections between
them. It's the place to think about structure with your hands, the way you might with index
cards on a wall.

:::note[Evolving during the alpha]
The Canvas is one of the newer planning surfaces. Its core — cards, groups, text boxes, and
arrows — is stable, but tools and gestures may change between alpha builds.
:::

## Opening the Canvas

Open the [Scene Board](../scene-board/) for a manuscript folder and switch to the Canvas. The
layout you create is **saved per folder**, so each part of your manuscript keeps its own
arrangement and you can return to exactly where you left off.

## Cards and groups

- **Cards** are your scenes. Drag them anywhere; their position is remembered.
- **Groups** (frames) gather related cards — an act, a sequence, a set of alternatives. A
  folder can expand inline on the canvas to show its children in place.
- Moving a card on the canvas is about *spatial* arrangement; it doesn't change your
  manuscript order unless you reorder it deliberately.

## Text boxes and arrows

The canvas isn't just cards:

- **Text boxes** let you drop headings, reminders, and free notes between your scenes.
- **Arrows** connect cards — handy for tracing a setup to its payoff, marking cause and
  effect, or sketching how subplots weave together.

## Selecting and arranging

- Click a card to select it; **lasso** a region to select several at once.
- A **selection toolbar** acts on everything you've picked — align, group, or move together.
- Multi-select makes it quick to reorganize a whole sequence in one gesture.

## Navigating the canvas

- **Pan** by dragging the background and **zoom** to move between the big picture and the
  detail.
- **Search** the canvas to jump straight to a card by title in a large board.
- View controls help you fit everything to the screen or return to a known zoom level.
