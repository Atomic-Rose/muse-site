The **Characters** view is a workspace for the people in your story — their point of view,
their profiles, and the scenes they appear in. Characters are a special kind of
[story thread](../story-threads/), so everything you tag here also feeds the Scene Board and
Story Lines.

:::note[Evolving during the alpha]
The Characters workspace and point-of-view tools are actively being developed. The ideas
here are stable; specific controls may change between alpha builds.
:::

## The Characters workspace

Open **Characters** from the editor's view switcher to see your cast in one place. From here
you can add a new character, open a character's profile, and see how each one moves through
the manuscript. Each character is backed by a thread, so its color and status are shared
everywhere it appears.

## Point of view

Mark the **point-of-view character** for a scene and MUSE can show POV across your manuscript —
useful for checking the balance of a multi-POV novel or making sure a chapter doesn't drift
between heads. POV is tracked as scene metadata, so you can filter and group by it on the
[Scene Board](../scene-board/) and in the [Outliner](../outliner/).

## Character profiles

A character can be linked to a **profile document** in your binder — a character bible holding
appearance, voice, history, and anything else you keep. When a character has a profile, MUSE
surfaces a banner so you can jump straight to it; when one doesn't yet, it prompts you to
create one. Start from a built-in template like *Character Profile* or *Deep Character
Profile* (see [Templates & guidance](../templates-and-guidance/)).

## Scene participation

For any scene you can record **which characters appear** in it, and optionally the role each
one plays. That participation drives the Characters lens on the Scene Board, so you can answer
questions like "which scenes is this character actually in?" or "where do these two finally
meet?" at a glance.
