**Compiling** assembles the documents in your binder into a single finished manuscript and
exports it to a standard format. Your individual scenes and chapters stay separate while you
work; compile stitches them together on demand.

This is an early draft written during the alpha. More detail and screenshots are coming.
:::

## What compile does

1. **Gathers** the documents you've marked to include.
2. **Applies your review choices** — accept tracked changes, reject them, or keep them as
   tracked changes in the output.
3. **Cleans up styling** — removes on-screen-only styling while keeping meaningful formatting
   like bold and italic.
4. **Assembles** front matter, chapters (with headings, numbering, and scene separators),
   and back matter.
5. **Exports** to your chosen format.

## Formats

- **DOCX** — Word document, optionally with tracked changes and comments preserved. Best for
  sending to editors and agents.
- **PDF** — a print-ready, page-formatted document.
- **Markdown** — plain, portable text.

## Presets and overrides

A compile **preset** captures all your settings — separator style, chapter numbering, fonts,
margins, and front/back matter. Use a built-in preset or save your own.

Individual documents can override the preset: a custom title, skip numbering, skip the
heading, a different separator, "compile as-is," or "start a new part."

For a step-by-step walkthrough, open the **Compile** dialog in the app and follow the panels
left to right.
