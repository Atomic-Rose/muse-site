[Install & first launch](../../alpha/install/).

## Create your first project

When MUSE opens, you'll see the **welcome screen**.

1. Choose **New Project**, then pick a name and a location on your computer for the
   `.muse` file. Your entire project lives in that single file — you can move, copy, or
   back it up like any other document.
2. Optionally pick a **starting template**. Templates add a few suggested folders and
   gentle writing prompts you're free to keep, ignore, or delete.
3. MUSE opens your new project in the main workspace.

Already have a manuscript elsewhere? You can bring it in instead — see
[Importing your work](../importing/) for Scrivener and Word projects.

:::tip[One project, one file]
Everything — your chapters, notes, research, comments, and history — lives in that one
`.muse` file. To back it up yourself at any time, just copy the file. MUSE also keeps
[automatic backups](../backups-and-safety/) for you.
:::

## The workspace

MUSE is built around three columns, with a toolbar across the top and a status bar along
the bottom.

- **The binder (left).** Your project's table of contents — a tree of folders and
  documents you can drag to reorder. This is how you navigate your manuscript. See
  [The binder](../the-binder/).
- **The editor (center).** Where you write. It can show a single document, several
  documents stitched together, a planning board, or a spreadsheet-style outline. See
  [The editor](../the-editor/).
- **The right panel.** Context for whatever you're working on. The **Inspector** holds the
  current document's synopsis, notes, metadata, writing goal, and links; other tabs hold
  the document **outline**, **comments**, **suggestions**, **footnotes**, and **version
  history**.

The **status bar** at the bottom shows your save state, word count, and writing goal
progress. You can hide the binder or the right panel at any time to widen your writing
space.

## Switching views

The same documents in your binder can be shown in several ways. Use the view switcher to
move between:

- **Editor** — write a document (or several) as prose.
- **[Scene Board](../scene-board/)** — a visual planning surface of index cards.
- **[Outliner](../outliner/)** — a spreadsheet of your documents with synopses and metadata.
- **[Research](../research-workspace/)** — collect and read source material.
- **[Characters](../characters-and-pov/)** — manage characters, point of view, and profiles.

## Write your first words

1. In the binder, select a document (or create one with the **+** button).
2. Click into the editor and start typing.
3. MUSE **saves automatically** as you write — there's no save button to remember. The
   status bar shows when your work is saved.

## Find your way around fast

A few tools help you move through a large project quickly:

- **Command palette** — type to run any command or jump to a feature.
- **Quick open** — jump straight to a document by name. See
  [Command palette & quick open](../command-palette/).
- **[Project search](../project-search/)** — full-text search across everything.
- **Keyboard shortcuts** — every action has a shortcut, and they're all listed in the
  in-app cheat sheet. You can remap any of them — see
  [Keyboard shortcuts](../keyboard-shortcuts/).

## Your work stays yours

MUSE is offline-first. Nothing is uploaded, there's no account, and there's no telemetry.
Your safety net is built in:

- **[Automatic backups](../backups-and-safety/)** run quietly as you work.
- **[Version snapshots](../version-history/)** let you roll a document back to an earlier draft.
- **[Crash recovery](../crash-recovery/)** restores unsaved work if MUSE closes unexpectedly.

See [Privacy](../../privacy/) for the full picture.
