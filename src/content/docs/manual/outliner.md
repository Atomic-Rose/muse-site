title: The Outliner
description: A spreadsheet view of your manuscript — synopses and metadata in sortable, filterable columns.
---

The **Outliner** shows your documents as **rows in a spreadsheet**, with their synopses and
metadata laid out in columns. Where the [Scene Board](../scene-board/) is for seeing your
story, the Outliner is for working through it methodically — scanning status, spotting short
scenes, and editing summaries in bulk. Switch to it from the editor's view switcher.

## Columns

Each row is a document. Columns can include its **title**, **synopsis**, **word count**,
**label**, **status**, **compile inclusion**, and any of your **custom metadata** fields.
Choose which columns are visible so the view shows exactly what you're working on — outline
status during planning, word counts during drafting, revision metadata during edits.

## Sorting and filtering

- **Sort** by any column — title, word count, status, or modified date — to bring the rows
  you care about to the top.
- **Filter** by label, status, compile inclusion, custom metadata, or revision state to focus
  on a slice of the manuscript.
- A quick **text filter** narrows rows by what you type.

## Editing inline

The Outliner is editable, not just a report. Click to rename a document, write or revise its
**synopsis**, change its **label** or **status**, and set **metadata** values — all without
opening the document. It's the fastest way to write synopses for a whole chapter in one pass.

## Bulk actions

Select multiple rows to act on them together — handy for setting a status across a set of
scenes or moving them as a group.

## Saved views

Save a configuration of **columns, sort, and filters** as a named **view** you can switch
between — for example a "Drafting" view that shows word counts and a "Revision" view that
shows status and revision metadata. (Free-text search stays out of saved views, since it's a
momentary query rather than part of a reusable layout.)

---

The Outliner, [Scene Board](../scene-board/), and [Canvas](../canvas/) are three lenses on the
same documents — a change in one shows up in all of them.
