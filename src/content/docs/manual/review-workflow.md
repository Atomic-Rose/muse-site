MUSE has a complete review workflow for revising — whether you're editing your own work or
incorporating notes.

:::note[Draft page]
This is an early draft written during the alpha. More detail and screenshots are coming.
:::

## Tracked changes (suggesting mode)

Turn on **suggesting mode** and your edits are recorded as suggestions — insertions and
deletions you can review later — rather than changing the text outright. Accept or reject
each change individually, or all at once.

## Revision passes

A **revision pass** is a named round of review (for example, "Line edit" or "Continuity
pass"). Scope a pass to the whole project, a folder, or a collection; every suggestion you
make while it's active is tagged to that pass. When you complete a pass, MUSE automatically
snapshots the documents it touched, so you always have a record of how things looked before.

## Comments

Select text and add a **comment** anchored to that spot. Comments support threaded replies —
useful for notes to yourself or back-and-forth with an editor or beta reader.

## Snapshots (version history)

Take a **snapshot** to freeze a document's current state. Snapshots let you compare against
or roll back to an earlier version at any time. You can **lock** important snapshots so
they're never automatically pruned. MUSE keeps the most recent snapshots per document
automatically.

## How it fits together

A typical revision cycle:

1. Create a revision pass.
2. Edit in suggesting mode — your changes are tracked and tagged to the pass.
3. Review the suggestions, accepting or rejecting each.
4. Complete the pass — MUSE snapshots the affected documents.
5. Archive the pass when you're done.
