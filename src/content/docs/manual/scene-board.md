title: The Scene Board
description: Plan and rearrange your manuscript visually with index cards, filters, lenses, and saved views.
---

The **Scene Board** shows the documents in a folder as **index cards** — a calm, visual way
to see the shape of your story, rearrange scenes, and spot gaps without scrolling through
prose. It's one of the editor's views; switch to it from the view switcher with a manuscript
folder selected.

:::note[Evolving during the alpha]
The planning surfaces are an area of active development. The cards, filters, and views
described here are stable, but exact layout and options may shift between alpha builds.
:::

## Cards

Each card represents one document and shows its **title**, **synopsis**, and any **label**
or **status** you've assigned. You can:

- **Rename** a card's title inline.
- **Edit the synopsis** directly on the card — the same one-line summary that appears in the
  Inspector and Outliner.
- **Reorder** cards by dragging; the order is your manuscript order.

Choose a **card size** (small, medium, or large) to trade detail for overview.

## Corkboard and Canvas

The Scene Board has two ways to lay cards out:

- A **corkboard / list** arrangement that follows your binder order — best for reading and
  reordering a chapter at a glance.
- A free-form **[Canvas](../canvas/)** — a pannable, zoomable space where you can place
  cards anywhere, group them, and draw connections. The Canvas is the home for spatial
  planning and is covered on its own page.

## Filtering and grouping

Open the **View** popover to narrow what the board shows. You can filter by:

- **Label** and **status**
- **Compile inclusion** (included / excluded)
- **Custom metadata** values
- **Revision** state (for example, items in the active pass, or with open comments)

Active filters are summarized on the View button, so you always know how much you've
narrowed the board.

## Signals

Turn on a **signal** to surface a quiet marker on cards that match a condition, without
changing your layout:

- **Readiness** — how finished a scene looks.
- **Recency** — what you've touched lately.
- **Target gap** — scenes short of a word-count target.

Signals are opt-in and stay out of the way until you ask for them.

## Lenses

A **lens** overlays story information on top of your cards:

- **[Characters](../characters-and-pov/)** — focus on point of view and who appears where.

## Saved views

Once you've set up filters, a lens, card size, and grouping the way you like, save it as a
**view** you can return to with one click — for example a "Drafting" view and a "Revision"
view. Saved views are remembered per folder, and you can rename, duplicate, or delete them.

## Creating and moving scenes

- **New Scene** adds a document to the current folder.
- Select one or more cards to reveal **bulk actions**, including **Move to folder**, so you
  can reorganize chapters without leaving the board.

## Keyboard and accessibility

The Scene Board is fully keyboard-navigable: cards take focus, the selected card is announced
to screen readers, and bulk actions, creation, renaming, and moves announce their results.
You can reach the board through the region-focus cycle along with the binder and panels.
