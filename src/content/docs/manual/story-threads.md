---
title: Story threads and Story Lines
description: Track characters, subplots, clues, and payoffs as they run through your scenes.
---

A **story thread** is anything that runs *through* your story rather than sitting in one
place — a character, a subplot, a recurring clue, a theme. MUSE lets you define these threads
and tag the scenes each one touches, so you can follow a subplot from setup to payoff or check
that a clue is actually planted before it pays off.

:::note[Evolving during the alpha]
Threads and the Story Lines overlay are a newer planning feature. The concepts here are
stable; specific controls and chips may change between alpha builds.
:::

## Story threads

Create a thread and give it a **type**, a **color**, and an optional description. Types
include:

- **Character**
- **Subplot**
- **Relationship**
- **Clue**
- **Theme**
- **Location**
- **Object**
- **Custom** (anything else you want to track)

Each thread has a **status** — *active*, *dormant*, or *resolved* — so you can tell at a
glance which threads are still in play. **Character** threads can also point to a profile
document in your binder (see [Characters & POV](../characters-and-pov/)).

## Assigning threads to scenes

Link a thread to a scene and give the link a **role** that says what the scene does for that
thread:

- **Appears** — the thread is simply present.
- **Setup** — a promise is planted.
- **Escalation** — the stakes rise.
- **Reversal** — the situation turns.
- **Reveal** — something hidden comes to light.
- **Payoff** — the promise is kept.
- **Resolution** — the thread is brought to a close.

You can add a short note to each link. With threads assigned, the [Scene Board](../scene-board/)
can overlay them so you can see which threads run through each scene.

## Promise and payoff

Beyond per-scene roles, you can record an explicit **promise → payoff** link between two
scenes: where a setup is planted and where it lands. Each link has a status — *planned*,
*resolved*, or *dropped* — which makes it easy to find promises you've made to the reader but
haven't yet kept.

## The Story Lines overlay

**Story Lines** is the unified overlay that brings threads together on the Scene Board. Filter
it with chips to focus on what matters right now:

- **All** threads, or just **characters**, **subplots**, **themes**, **clues**, **locations**,
  **objects**, **relationships**, or **custom** threads.
- Optionally include **dormant and resolved** threads, or hide them to see only what's active.

It's a quick way to ask "where does this subplot show up?" or "which scenes is this character
actually in?" without leaving your planning view.
