The **binder** is the left-hand panel — your project's table of contents. It holds every
document in your project as a tree you can rearrange freely, and it's where you'll spend
most of your time navigating.

## Documents, folders, and research

The binder holds three kinds of items:

- **Folders** group related material — for example, *Part One* or *Characters*.
- **Text documents** are the things you write in: chapters, scenes, notes.
- **Research** items hold reference material — see [The Research workspace](../research-workspace/).

Folders and documents are more alike than they look: a text document can itself contain
other documents, so a chapter can hold its own scenes without becoming a "folder." You can
nest items as deeply as you like.

Create new items with the **+** button or the right-click menu, and rename an item by
double-clicking it.

## Root folders

The top level of the binder is made of **root folders**. Two are always present and can't
be removed:

- **Manuscript** — the text that becomes your finished book.
- **Trash** — where deleted items wait (see below).

You can turn on additional roots as you need them — **Research**, **Characters**,
**Locations**, **Worldbuilding**, **Notes**, and **Templates**. Hiding a root never
deletes its contents; everything reappears if you turn it back on. Root folders are
protected — they can't be renamed, moved, or dragged into one another. See
[Root folders](../root-folders/) for the full list and how to enable them.

## Reordering and nesting

Drag any item to move it:

- Drop **between** two items to reorder.
- Drop **onto** a folder (or a document) to nest inside it.
- Hover over a collapsed folder while dragging and it **expands automatically** so you can
  drop deeper.

The binder updates instantly, and binder moves are **undoable**. A few safety rules keep
things sane: you can't drop an item into its own descendants, and you can't drag the
protected root folders.

## Labels, statuses, keywords, and metadata

Right-click a document (or use the Inspector) to tag it:

- **Labels** — a single colored tag per document, shown as a dot in the binder. Good for
  POV character, storyline, or anything you want to see at a glance.
- **Statuses** — where a document stands, shown as a badge: *Outline*, *Draft*, *Revised*,
  and so on.
- **Keywords** — free-form tags; a document can have as many as you like.
- **Custom metadata** — your own fields (text, dropdown, multi-select, date, number, or
  checkbox).

These tags power filtering, the [Scene Board](../scene-board/), the
[Outliner](../outliner/), and [collections](../collections/). See
[Labels, statuses & keywords](../labels-statuses-keywords/) and
[Custom metadata](../metadata/) to set them up.

## Backlink badges

When other documents link to the one you're looking at, the binder shows a small **backlink
count** on hover. It's a quick way to see how connected a character, place, or scene is.
Links come from the Inspector and from `[[wikilinks]]` in your text — see
[Wikilinks & backlinks](../wikilinks-and-backlinks/).

## Collections

**Collections** are saved groupings of documents that appear in their own section of the
sidebar:

- A **smart collection** gathers every document matching rules you set (for example,
  *status is Draft and label is Subplot B*) and updates itself as your project changes.
- A **static collection** is a hand-picked list you curate yourself.

See [Collections](../collections/) for how to build them.

## Other views of the binder

The binder is one way to see your documents; the editor's view switcher offers others. The
**[Scene Board](../scene-board/)** shows the same items as index cards on a corkboard or a
spatial canvas, and the **[Outliner](../outliner/)** shows them as rows in a spreadsheet.
They're different lenses on the same tree — changes in one show up everywhere.

## Trash

Deleting an item moves it to **Trash** rather than destroying it, and MUSE remembers where
it came from so you can **restore** it to its original place. Items in the trash are styled
faintly so you can tell them apart.

Nothing leaves your project permanently until you choose **Empty Trash**, which asks you to
confirm. For more on protecting your work, see [Backups & safety](../backups-and-safety/).
