The **binder** is the left-hand panel — your project's table of contents. It holds every
document in your project as a tree of folders and files you can rearrange freely.

## Documents and folders

The binder contains three kinds of items:

- **Folders** — containers for grouping (for example, "Part One" or "Characters").
- **Text documents** — the things you write in: chapters, scenes, notes.
- **Research** — reference material you want to keep alongside your manuscript.

Create new items with the **+** button or the right-click menu. Rename by double-clicking.

## Reordering and nesting

Drag any item to move it. Drop it onto a folder to nest it inside, or between items to
reorder. The binder updates instantly, and every move is undoable.

## Labels, statuses, and metadata

Right-click a document to tag it:

- **Labels** — colored tags (for example, by POV character or storyline).
- **Statuses** — where a document stands (for example, *Outline*, *Draft*, *Revised*).
- **Custom metadata** — your own fields (text, dropdown, date, number, or checkbox).

These tags power filtering, the Scene Board, and smart collections.

## Collections

**Collections** are saved views of your project. A *smart collection* gathers every document
that matches rules you set (for example, "status is Draft AND label is Subplot B"), and
updates itself as your project changes.

## The Scene Board and Outliner

From the editor's view switcher you can see your binder as:

- **The Scene Board** — a visual planning surface with seven modes (Scenes, Freeform,
  Organize, Canvas, Structure, Timeline, Connections).
- **The Outliner** — a spreadsheet-style view of your documents with their synopses and
  metadata in columns.

Both are different lenses on the same documents in your binder.

## Trash

Deleted items move to the **Trash** rather than disappearing. Empty the trash deliberately
when you're sure. Nothing leaves your project until you choose to remove it.
