The **editor** is the center panel — where you actually write. It's built to stay responsive
even on book-length documents, and it keeps the tools you need within reach without cluttering
the page.

## Writing and saving

Type to write. MUSE **saves automatically** as you go — there's no save button. The status
bar shows your save state and live word count, and on very large documents saving and
checking happen quietly in the background so they never interrupt your typing.

## Formatting and styles

A **format bar** gives you the essentials: bold, italic, headings, lists, alignment, block
quotes, links, highlights, and more. For consistent, reusable formatting you can define your
own **paragraph and character styles** rather than formatting by hand each time. See
[Formatting & styles](../formatting-and-styles/).

## Structure and rich content

Beyond plain prose, the editor supports the building blocks of a real manuscript:

- **Headings** that feed the document [outline](../outline-and-connections/).
- **Page breaks** and **section breaks** for controlling layout and headers.
- **Tables**, **images** you can resize in place, and **special characters**.
- A **print layout** that paginates your document the way it will appear on the page.

## Focus tools

When you want the world to fall away:

- **Focus mode** dims everything except the sentence or paragraph you're working on.
- **Zen mode** hides the surrounding panels for a full-screen, distraction-free page.
- **Typewriter scrolling** keeps the line you're writing centered on screen.
- **Syntax highlighting** can be toggled on to color parts of speech as you write.

See [Focus, Zen & typewriter](../focus-and-typewriter/).

## Wikilinks and backlinks

Type `[[` to link to another document by name, with autocomplete. Links are two-way: each
document's Inspector shows what links *to* it (its **backlinks**), so you can move through
your project like a web of connected notes. See
[Wikilinks & backlinks](../wikilinks-and-backlinks/).

## Footnotes and endnotes

Insert footnotes and endnotes directly in the text, edit them in a dedicated panel, and
choose how they appear when you compile. See [Footnotes & endnotes](../footnotes/).

## Spelling and grammar

MUSE checks your writing as you type and offers suggestions you can accept or dismiss:

- **Spelling**, with a custom dictionary you can add words to.
- **Repeated adjacent words** (e.g. "the the").
- **Very long sentences** that may be worth breaking up.
- **Wordy phrases** that have shorter equivalents.
- **Passive-voice** indicators.
- **Weak intensifiers and crutch words**, **adverbs**, and **clichés**.

Each check can be turned on or off, and on large documents checking runs in the background
so it never slows your typing.

## Templates and guidance

Create documents from **templates** — built-in ones like *Scene Builder*, *Chapter Builder*,
and *Character Profile*, or your own. Templates can carry optional **ghost-prompt guidance**:
faint, in-context prompts that suggest what to write in each section and disappear as soon as
you start typing. See [Templates & guidance](../templates-and-guidance/).

## Scrivenings and split view

Select a folder (or several documents) to edit them as one continuous flow — called
**Scrivenings** — while they stay separate files underneath. You can also **split** the
editor to show two documents side by side. See
[Scrivenings & split view](../scrivenings-and-split/).

## Read-aloud and lookup

Hear your prose read back to you to catch awkward phrasing, and look up any word in the
built-in **dictionary** and **thesaurus** without leaving the page. See
[Read-aloud & lookup](../read-aloud-and-lookup/).

## The Inspector and side panels

To the right of the editor:

- The **Inspector** holds the current document's **synopsis**, **notes**, **metadata**,
  **writing goal**, and **links**.
- A **synopsis bar** above the text shows (and lets you edit) the one-line summary used on
  Scene Board cards.
- Other tabs hold the document **outline**, **comments**, **suggestions** (tracked changes),
  **footnotes**, and **version history**.

## Writing goals and sessions

Set a word-count target for a document or the whole project and MUSE tracks your progress in
the status bar and Inspector, along with your writing sessions over time. See
[Writing goals](../writing-goals/) and [Sessions & statistics](../statistics/).

## Working with very large documents

The editor is engineered to stay smooth on 80,000-word manuscripts and beyond — heavy work
like word counting and pagination runs off the main thread. If a single document grows large
enough to risk slowing down, MUSE will gently suggest splitting it into smaller working
documents, which you can do safely without losing anything.
