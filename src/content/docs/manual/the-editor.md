The **editor** is the center panel — where you actually write. It's built to stay fast even
on very large documents.

:::note[Draft page]
This is an early draft written during the alpha. More detail and screenshots are coming.
:::

## Writing and formatting

Type to write. A formatting bar gives you the essentials — bold, italic, headings, lists,
alignment, links, and more. MUSE saves your work automatically as you go.

## Focus tools

- **Focus mode** — hide everything but your text for distraction-free writing.
- **Typewriter scrolling** — keep the line you're writing centered on screen.
- **Focus levels** — dim everything except the current sentence or paragraph.

## Scrivenings (multi-document editing)

Select a folder (or several documents) to edit them as one continuous flow, while they stay
separate files underneath. This is ideal for reading a chapter made of many scenes.

## Wikilinks

Type `[[` to link to another document by name, with autocomplete. Links are two-way: each
document's inspector shows what links *to* it (backlinks), so you can navigate your project
like a web of connected notes.

## Spelling and grammar

MUSE checks spelling as you type and offers grammar suggestions (repeated words, long
sentences, passive voice, wordiness). You can add words to a custom dictionary and toggle
checks on or off. On large documents, checking happens in the background so it never slows
your typing.

## Writing goals and sessions

Set a word-count target for a document or the project, and MUSE tracks your progress and
your writing sessions in the inspector — gentle motivation without nagging.
