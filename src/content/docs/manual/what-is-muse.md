MUSE (Manuscript Unified Studio Environment) is a desktop writing application for people
who write long things: novelists, screenwriters, and researchers. It combines
Scrivener-style project organization with a modern editor, single-file `.muse` projects,
and a complete review workflow.

The goal is an app that feels **calm, trustworthy, and focused** — somewhere you can keep
an entire book and actually enjoy working in it.

## What you can do with MUSE

- **Organize a whole book.** A binder tree holds chapters, scenes, characters, places, and
  research side by side. Reorder by dragging. Tag documents with labels, statuses, and your
  own custom metadata.
- **Plan visually.** The Scene Board offers seven views of your project — Scenes, Freeform,
  Organize, Canvas, Structure, Timeline, and Connections — so you can see your story from
  whatever angle helps.
- **Draft comfortably.** Write in a rich editor designed for very large documents, with
  focus mode, typewriter scrolling, wikilinks between documents, and live spelling and
  grammar suggestions.
- **Revise thoroughly.** Turn on suggesting mode to track changes, group edits into named
  revision passes, leave anchored comments, and take snapshots you can return to.
- **Compile and export.** Assemble selected documents into a finished manuscript and export
  to DOCX (with tracked changes), PDF, or Markdown.

## What makes it different

- **One file, on your computer.** Each project is a single `.muse` file (a SQLite
  database). There is no cloud account and nothing is uploaded. This avoids the sync
  corruption that plagues file-and-folder writing setups.
- **Built for big manuscripts.** The editor is engineered to stay responsive on 80,000-word
  documents and beyond.
- **Trust first.** An automatic backup system, version snapshots, and crash-recovery drafts
  exist so you never have to worry about losing your work.

## What MUSE is *not* (yet)

MUSE is in **private alpha**. It is feature-rich but still early: expect occasional bugs and
interface details that will change. During alpha, please don't make MUSE the only home for
irreplaceable work — always keep copies of anything you cannot afford to lose.
